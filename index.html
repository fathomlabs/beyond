Kenya

Kenya is a beautiful country in East Africa with a wonderfully diverse flora and fauna. It provides a wealth of amazing sights and experiences to the hundreds of thousands of tourists visiting each year. However it is also the home to some of the poorest people in the world. It is not immediately clear why poverty persists in spite of the presence of a highly lucrative tourism business.

Maasai warrior

One reason for the persistence of poverty in Kenya is that the proceeds of tourism often leave the country with local people never seeing them. Many assets (land, cars, campsites) used in the tourism trade are owned outside of Kenya. These are often rented out to local guides at very high rates meaning that they are left with a tiny salary after paying. This creates a poverty trap whereby the locals are unable to build a secure future for themselves and their families no matter how hard they work; and the (vast) proceeds of tourism are never seen by locals as they are exported immediately.

Maasai Mara national park

Fathom Beyond seeks to ameliorate the issue of exploitation of local workers in the tourism business by keeping the proceeds of tourism in the local area. In this scenario people can become empowered to build themselves a secure future and support their families. Initially we will work with a local guide and rent amenities at a fair rate, allowing guides to build up their own capital. We will then invest our net profits back into the project in order to bring a fair deal to more local people.
