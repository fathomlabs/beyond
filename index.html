Fathom Beyond

Fathom Beyond is an ethical tourism company, working in the Maasai Mara in Kenya. Our primary aim is to keep the profits of tourism in the hands of local Maasai communities, some of the poorest people in the world. We work with safari guides, scientists, and naturalists to provide a quality experience to tourists, whilst taking care of the natural world.

The Maasai Mara is perhaps the most famous and popular tourist destination in Kenya. It generates an estimated Â£13 million each year from its hundreds of thousands of visitors. However its local residents, the Maasai people, are some of the poorest in the world. One study found that 55% of adults in a Maasai tribe were wasted or underweight, and 36% of infants were stunted. Poverty is rife: around 70% of Maasai communities are poor despite investment in education and employment. Many tour operators are run by foreigners, and see local people only as cheap labour, offering them with only a tiny fraction of the proceeds. Existing eco-tourism efforts are investing in important initiatives, but many are failing to work with local people to build on the skills and entrepreneurship that is already present.
