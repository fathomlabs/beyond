Kenya

Kenya is a beautiful country in East Africa with a wonderfully diverse flora and fauna. It provides a wealth of amazing sights and experiences to the hundreds of thousands of tourists visiting each year. However it is also the home to some of the poorest people in the world. It is not immediately clear why poverty persists in spite of the presence of a highly lucrative tourism business.

Maasai Mara national park

Unfortunately the profits of tourism often leave Kenya without benefitting the local citizens. Many assets (safari vehicles for example) used in the tourism trade are owned outside of Kenya and can be rented out to local tour guides at very high rates leaving them with an effectively tiny salary. This creates a poverty trap whereby locals are unable to build a secure future for themselves and their families no matter how hard they work; the vast proceeds of tourism are never seen as they are exported immediately.

Maasai people

Fathom Beyond will help local Maasai guides to build self-sufficiency and financial security. We will reduce the exploitation of local workers in the tourism business in the Maasai Mara in Kenya by keeping the proceeds of their business local. Initially we will work with local guides, purchasing and renting out safari vehicles at a fair rate, allowing them to build up their own capital. We will then invest our net profits back into the project in order to bring a fair deal to more local people.
